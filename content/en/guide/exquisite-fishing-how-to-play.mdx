### The Five Pillars of Fishing
1.  **Preparation**: Before leaving the dock, you must visit the shop to equip a **Hook** and a **Gift**. Without the correct combination, special creatures like the Mermaid or Shark will ignore your lure.
2.  **Casting**: Launch your lure into the ocean. You have manual control over the horizontal movement as the lure sinks.
3.  **Attraction**: Guide your lure toward a target. If you have the correct gear, a meter will appear above the creature. You must stay near them until the meter fills.
4.  **Reeling**: Once hooked, you must reel the line back to the surface. Some high-tier hooks make this faster or provide "shields" against losing the catch.
5.  **Economy**: Sell your catch at the end of the run. Use this gold to buy better boats, which are the primary "keys" to unlocking deeper ocean biomes.

<Callout type="tip" title="Beginner Strategy">
In the very beginning, don't waste gold on random gifts. Use the **Regular Hook** and **Diamond Ring** to target the Mermaid at 50m. She is a consistent source of early-game income.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Gear Upgrades and Shop Priorities">

The shop in **Exquisite Fishing** is filled with various hooks and gifts. Understanding which ones to buy first is the difference between a 2-hour playthrough and a 10-hour grind. Hooks generally improve your depth capability and reeling efficiency, while gifts act as "bait" for specific characters.

### Hook Progression
Hooks are categorized by their "Shielding" and "Depth" capabilities. A **Double Shielded Hook** allows you to ignore small obstacles or mistakes during the reeling process, while the **Omega Hook** is required for the most extreme depths in the Dark Ocean.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Early Game Hooks">
    Stick with the **Regular Hook** and **Good Hook**. These are sufficient for the Calm and Rocky oceans. Focus your gold on the 1,500g boat upgrade first.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Mid-Game Pivot">
    Once you have the Regular Boat, purchase the **Reinforced Hook** and **Drill Hook**. These allow you to catch the Kappa (7,500g) and the Rocky Ocean Angler (13,000g).
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Endgame Specialization">
    In the Dark Ocean, you will need the **Anal Beads Hook** for the Octopus and the **Omega Hook** for the final 2,000m Angler. These are expensive but necessary for 100% completion.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Gold Farming Tip">
The **Reinforced Hook** is the best value-for-money upgrade in the game. It works for multiple targets across three different oceans, making it a "must-buy" early on.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Advanced Strategies for Gold Farming">

By the time you reach the late game, boat costs and high-tier hooks will require tens of thousands of gold. You cannot rely on catching common fish to make progress. You must optimize your "Gold Per Run" by targeting the most efficient creatures.

1.  **The Angler Loop**: In the Calm Ocean, the Angler at 300m pays 10,000g. Since the entry fee is 0g, this is the most efficient way to save up for your first 5,000g boat.
2.  **Turbulent Shark Farming**: Once you have the Reinforced Boat, the Shark at 900m pays 24,000g. Use the **Super Hook** and **Diamond Ring**. This is the fastest way to afford the Golden Boat.
3.  **Endgame Dark Ocean**: The final Angler at 2,000m pays 45,000g. While the entry fee is 8,000g, a single successful catch nets you a 37,000g profit.

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Common Fish">
In the late game, common fish are a distraction. They fill your inventory and provide very little gold. Focus entirely on special targets to maximize your time efficiency.
</Callout>
